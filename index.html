Programs

Your connection. Your way.

Our "General Penpairing" Program

Connecting penpals virtually together for social purposes.

1. For patients: you have the choice to match with either fellow patient penpals and/or non-patient penpals.
2. For non-patients: In preserving the sacredness of our program, you may only match with patient penpals.
3. For everyone: Send each other virtual letters, doodles, and gifts! You may only form two penpairs at the same time as we highly value the quality of our penpair relationships. (FAQ for more!)

Our "Research Penpairing" Program

Connecting penpals and medical professionals virtually together for research purposes.

1. For patient penpals: If you are open to it - you also have the choice to connect for research purposes.
2. For researchers: You MUST indicate that you are a research penpal (and not a general non-patient penpal). Due to our safety measures, you must fill out an application that verifies your research purpose.
3. For everyone: There are no limits to the number of research based penpairs.

Our "Live Penpairing" Program

Connecting penpals together in real life.

At Penpairs, we heavily monitor the exchange of personal information as our penpals' safety is highly important to us. However, we do understand the importance of in-person gatherings so please check periodically for supervised "Penpairs Meetups". These meetups are open to all Penpairs users. If you are interested in hosting a meetup in your locality, please see FAQ's.

Our "Sponsor Penpairing" Program

Connecting sponsors to patients in need.

1. For patients: If you seek donations, you may fill out and submit an application.
2. For sponsors: After viewing verified profiles, you may only donate the verified needed amount to your patient of choice.
3. For everyone: At Penpairs, 100% of Sponsor-A-Patient donations go directly to the sponsored patient.

Common Problems

    In general, most persons experience social isolation and loneliness at various stages of their lives. However, there are also more specific problems to address:
  • PATIENT ISOLATION:
    Patients have limited to no interaction with persons outside of the hospital, hospital volunteers are entirely domestic persons (also frequently disallowed due to health precautions), and lack of social skills leave survivors feeling isolated even after discharge.
  • GENERAL SOCIAL LONELINESS AND CULURAL TABOO:
    This is a common feeling among ALL persons (regardless of one's general health). Social loneliness and isolation have also been known to contribute to depression and suicide.
  • OVERPOLLUTED ONLINE MEDIUMS:
    An overpollution of penpaling, and dating sites with an overflux of random users, lack of safety in private information exchange, unclear intentions among users, and the consequence of quantity over quality due to unlimited amount of "matches".
  • LACK OF OPTIONS:
    In most cases, the only form to meet/help disadvantaged beings (for both altruistic and therapeutic reasons) is through in-person volunteering at select hospitals and shelters.
  • UNVERIFIED AND INDIRECT DONATIONS:
    There is the constant fear of being scammed when donating directly to patients. In addition, donating to large organizations gives one little autonomy in the donation's final destination.

Our Solutions

    We are fighting the stigma of shame that is associated with feeling lonely, and isolated by creating a medium that ALL persons can shamelessly access, share, and interact with others in a fun, engaging manner.
  • MYRIAD OF OPTIONS:
    Patients can choose to match with fellow patients penpals, researchers, and/or general non-patient penpals.
  • ENCOURAGEMENT OF DIVERSITY:
    Open to all ages, genders, ethnicities, etc.
  • ELIMINATING THE STIGMA:
    Unlike the social taboos that surround having a therapist, etc - sharing your penpal experience is an unique, shameless journey.
  • QUALITY OVER QUANTITY:
    General penpals may only form a max of 2 penpairs at a time so one can focus on nurturing their penpair relationship.
  • A SAFE HAVEN:
    Verified signup process, privatization of users' contact information, and heavy monitorization of personal information exchanges.
  • DIRECT AID:
    Sponsors only view verified stories of financially disadvantaged patients before donating the verified needed amount to their patient of choice.

Bridging the Gap

As a social enterprise, Penpairs is fighting the stigma of shame that is associated with feeling lonely and isolated. As humans, it is NORMAL to have such sentiments and it is in our nature to want to connect to those around us. And although everyone interprets this differently, sometimes having a listening ear is all it takes to brighten one's day.
